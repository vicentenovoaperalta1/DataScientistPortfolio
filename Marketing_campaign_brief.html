About the Project

The project aims to support a marketing campaign for a car company by predicting which existing customers are most likely to purchase a new car. The dataset used is owned by the company and its based on previous marketing campaigns. The machine learning model's results will be used to decide which customers to contact in the next marketing campaign.

Exploratory Data Analysis


Target Variable

Target Variable Distribution: There were a total of 131,337 clients contacted during the last campaign. The campaign achieved a 2.7% conversion rate. It is highly imbalanced, something that I will address later.

Distributions

Gender: We don't know the gender of 50% of the database. 27% are male and 20% female.

Age: We don't know the age of 85% of the database. Out of the rest, 80% are below the age of 54.

Car model: Models 2, 5, 3, 1, and 4 are the most prevalent, collectively comprising 80% of the sample.

Car segment: Car segments Small/Medium and Large/SUV are 40% of the data each, and LCV is 20%.

Rates Per Variable

Gender: Gender unknown has the highest conversion rate of 3.1%, followed by male with a 2.7% rate, doubling the female rate of 1.5%.

This highly indicates that gender is going to be a key variable for the campaign.

Age: The Unknown group has the highest conversion rate, and it represents the 85% of the data. Out of the rest, the best conversion rates seem to be around [35, 64].

Car segment: Car segments Small/Medium and Large/SUV have a 3.0% conversion rate, nearly twice that of the other two categories.

Correlation Heatmap

This heatmap shows the correlations between all the numerical variables in the dataset. Many of the independent variables have high correlations between them, so it's probably better not to include all of them in the ML model. Also, it shows that all the variables are negatively correlated to the target variable.

Model: Catboost

This model is very efficient at handling categorical variables. It works by building symmetric trees and doing ordered boosting to help avoid overfitting. It performs well with noisy data and small datasets.



ROC Curve

This model shows a very good ROC Curve and its goodness is confirmed by a ROCAUC = 99.4%.



Confusion Matrix

This heatmap shows the correlations between all the numerical variables in the dataset. Classification Report

The model achieves a 99% accuracy, with very good f1-scores of 99% and 81%, and succesfully predicts 89% of customers who will be interested in buying a new car.

Conclusion

This project delivers usefull results for the marketing campaign execution. It lessens the clients to be contacted from ~131.000 to ~800, while succesfully achieving 89% of the sales. This is if the model's results are used directly. Alternatively, the model also gives out probabilities for each customer, so the number of customers to be contacted can be customized.
